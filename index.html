1) Online Video Formats

Please give a brief description to demonstrate your familiarity with the following formats:

MP4: A classic and very versatile format. It is based on the Apple Quicktime format .mov.

HLS: A relatively new format, was created by Apple for use with iOS, Apple TV, and Mac later versions.

RTMP: Real Time Messaging Protocol is "a system for delivering on demand and live media to Adobe Flash Applications..." (quoted from the JW Player documentation). Drawbacks include vunerability to being blocked by firewalls and possible stream stuttering if the connection is dropped for any length of time.

FLV: Adobe Flash video. Has been around for a long time but is increasingly not supported in modern web browsers.

WEBM: WEBM is an open media format (open source sponsored by Google) whose core technologies are HTTP, HTML, and TCP/IP. Advantages include high efficiency bandwidth usage and the high quality of the streams, also good for use on low power devices.

2) Which of the above video formats do not work on mobile devices?

RTMP and FLV do not work on mobile devices.
